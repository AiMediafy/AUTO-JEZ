<h2>Anthropic debuts Project Glasswing: fast summaries of large document collections</h2><p>Glasswing is a new project from Anthropic, designed to automate the analysis and synthesis of documents – from reports and audits to contracts. In practice, this means you can upload a set of files (Word, PDF, email, etc.) and receive concise, automated analysis without the need for manual labor from the administrative or legal departments.</p><h2>What does it mean in practice?</h2><ul><li><strong>Glasswing integrates with Claude</strong> – Anthropic’s latest model, supporting up to 200,000 tokens of context. That’s quite a lot for complex documents.</li><li><strong>API interface</strong> allows you to connect the tool to your own systems – ready-made adapters are already available for popular platforms such as Slack, Gmail, or Notion.</li><li><strong>Summaries and recommendations</strong> are generated in an easily auditable format (e.g., a dashboard for quick review and comparison of the main threads).</li><li><strong>Security and compliance</strong>: Anthropic declares robust data protection mechanisms – it’s worth verifying this in practice, especially for regulated sectors.</li></ul><h2>Real numbers: is it worth it?</h2><p>From my perspective: if a company processes 40+ documents per week, with each analysis taking an average of 30 minutes (e.g., contracts, offers, audits) – in a team of 3 people, that can mean <strong>60 hours of work weekly</strong>. At a rate of 80 PLN/hour, this amounts to about <strong>19–20 thousand PLN monthly</strong>. Automating even half the process represents a real saving of around 10 thousand PLN/month and frees resources for more substantive tasks.</p><h2>Challenges and opportunities</h2><ul><li><strong>Data needs to be well-organized</strong>: the tool won’t resolve chaos in folders or messes in file versioning.</li><li><strong>Glasswing is in the pilot phase</strong>: the actual quality of generated summaries depends on how well Anthropic’s models handle imperfect data (I’ve seen similar promises in previous years – key factors will be ergonomics and resilience to non-standard formats).</li><li><strong>The real value</strong> emerges when you can automate not just summaries, but also workflows – such as automatic classification, risk scoring, or alert distribution.</li></ul><h2>Conclusions</h2><p>Anthropic’s Glasswing looks like a very interesting step toward practical automation of document analysis. It’s not a “magic wand” just yet – companies with well-organized processes and high repeatability will benefit most. It’s worth testing, asking about compatibility with your own infrastructure, and comparing with other tools (OpenAI, Google).</p><p>Let me know in the comments if you’d like to share your experience or if you’re interested in implementation details – I’ll gladly provide a concrete cost and ROI analysis.</p>
