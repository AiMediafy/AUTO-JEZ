<h2>AI in Investments: Balyasny Asset Management with GPT-5.4</h2><p>The team at Balyasny Asset Management has just unveiled its approach to using AI in investment analysis. A key aspect here is the application of GPT-5.4 from OpenAI, a next-generation model supported by advanced model evaluation and automated agent workflows. <strong>This is not just an experiment – it's a transformation in scale and operation.</strong></p><h2>What does it mean in practice?</h2><p>First and foremost, we are talking about the integration of an AI system at the level of strategic investment decisions. The implementation includes:</p><ul><li>GPT-5.4 model – enhanced compared to its predecessors, geared toward data analysis and complex decision-making processes.</li><li>Rigorous model evaluation – to ensure that AI supports genuinely accurate decisions. This is crucial in an environment where the margin for error must be minimal.</li><li>Automated agent workflows – processes where AI acts as an active “advisor,” suggesting, analyzing, and optimizing.</li></ul><p>In practice, this means that much of the routine and analytical work (e.g., sifting through reports, analyzing macro- and microeconomic data, or detecting trends) is supported by AI. Time savings and operational efficiency can be significant here.</p><h2>Real value: what can be gained?</h2><p>Let's assume an investment data analysis team consists of 20 people, each spending about 15 hours per week on research. At a rate of 100 PLN per hour, this amounts to 120,000 PLN in monthly labor costs. Even if AI reduces the workload by 30%, we are talking about savings of approximately 36,000 PLN per month – which, given the cost of implementing AI, starts to look like a worthwhile investment.</p><p>From my perspective, the key here is not only the savings but also the improvement in decision quality. AI can detect dependencies and trends that even experienced analysts might miss, working on massive amounts of data in real-time.</p><h2>Conclusions</h2><p>Using AI, especially in sensitive areas like investments, requires a combination of robust models, process integration, and human expertise.
