<p><strong>OpenAI has just published a practical guide showing how ChatGPT, Codex, and their API are actually being used in business and everyday tasks.</strong> This is not a festival of promises – the company showcases real use cases that are already being adopted by hundreds of thousands of users.</p>

<h2>What does this really mean?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>ChatGPT</strong>: Automates customer service, creates document summaries, generates marketing content, enables faster data analysis. The key: instant access via a chat interface – even for people without a technical background.</li>
  <li><strong>Codex</strong>: AI for developers – generates code, translates code snippets, helps with debugging. In practice: time savings, fewer repetitive tasks, faster onboarding of new devs.</li>
  <li><strong>OpenAI API</strong>: Integrates AI into your own products (e.g., CRM, helpdesk, HR systems), enables full automation of processes – from on-demand text generation to document analysis.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Technically – what does implementation provide?</h2>
<p>From my perspective, all the magic starts when companies combine their own databases and processes with the OpenAI API. A typical scenario: automatic query handling (e.g., 15 hours per week × 4 people × 60 PLN/hour = around 14–15 thousand PLN saved monthly), report generation, or support in recruitment.</p>
<p><strong>However, the key is:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Good data preparation (clean, up-to-date, well-documented)</li>
  <li>Processes that can actually be automated (not every routine suits AI)</li>
  <li>Sensible team onboarding – AI is just a tool, you need to know how to use it</li>
  <li>Infrastructure – fast servers, robust API, backups, monitoring</li>
</ul>

<h2>Challenges – no sugarcoating</h2>
<p>I've already seen companies that have gone from testing to real-world use, but the key turned out not to be "add a chatbot" but to teach people how to use it and take care of the processes. The biggest challenges? Data quality and integration with existing systems. Without this, AI performs mediocrely.</p>

<h2>Business numbers</h2>
<p>With a well-done implementation, automating even 20% of repetitive tasks already brings tangible savings – 2–4 people relieved, rapid document generation, better analytics. In clear mathematics: <strong>from several to even tens of thousands of PLN monthly.</strong></p>

<h2>Summary</h2>
<p><strong>Strong OpenAI use cases are no longer just demos – this is real automation for companies.</strong> But for it to work: data, processes, onboarding. I've seen similar promises before, and the crucial part was always the "boring" organizational details.</p>
