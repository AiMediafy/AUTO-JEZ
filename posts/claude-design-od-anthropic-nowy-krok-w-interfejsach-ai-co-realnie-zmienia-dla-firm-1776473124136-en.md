<h2>Anthropic presents Claude Design. What's next for AI interfaces?</h2> <p>Today Anthropic unveiled <a href="https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-design-anthropic-labs">Claude Design</a> – a solution intended to let users design and edit documents, graphics, or tables directly within an AI environment. For the industry: another step toward more visual and interactive interfaces. In my opinion: a necessary move, seeing how strongly the market is shifting toward assistants that combine text, images, tables, and workflows – not just text-based chats.</p> <h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2> <ul> <li><strong>Not just text:</strong> Claude Design allows for generating and modifying content in various formats. This is a significant evolution from conversational chatbots toward "multimodal" tools.</li> <li><strong>Real-time collaboration:</strong> Multiple people can edit a single "project" – in practice, it resembles Google Docs or Notion, only powered by AI.</li> <li><strong>Responsiveness to business needs:</strong> Direct creation and editing of charts, schedules, or presentation drafts. Faster than traditional tools – though, of course, the outcome depends on the quality of prompts and model training.</li> <li><strong>Security and compliance:</strong> Anthropic declares that Claude Design uses the same security solutions as the rest of the Claude ecosystem – this is crucial when deploying in companies that require a high level of data control.</li> </ul> <h2>Examples of benefits – how much can it actually deliver?</h2> <p>Suppose a 4-person team prepares a weekly report (data + graphics + summary):</p> <ul> <li>Today: 16 h/week × 80 PLN/h (average gross labor cost) = about 5,000 PLN per month.</li> <li>With Claude Design: even if you reduce the time by 40% (a realistic saving from implementation experience), you save about 2,000 PLN/month on a single process – plus faster corrections, fewer errors, and a better workflow in the team.</li> </ul> <h2>Challenges: technology won't replace the process</h2> <ul> <li><strong>Input data:</strong> If your data sources are messy – even the best AI can't build a good chart.</li> <li><strong>User education:</strong> A new interface means a change in the way of working – it's worth planning for onboarding and training.</li> <li><strong>API and integrations:</strong> For businesses, it's crucial to connect Claude Design with existing systems. At the moment there are lots of promises – in my experience, this is key to avoid work "islands."</li> </ul> <h2>Conclusions + what next?</h2> <p><strong>Claude Design</strong> confirms the direction: AI must be a tool, not just a conversational partner. The key, however, will be smart implementation – technologies won't organize data on their own or motivate people to change habits. From my side: it's worth testing, but think about the whole process.</p> <p>Do you have questions about deploying such solutions in your company?
