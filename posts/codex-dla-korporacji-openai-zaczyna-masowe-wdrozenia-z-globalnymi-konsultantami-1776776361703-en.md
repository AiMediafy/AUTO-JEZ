<p><strong>OpenAI is launching a major partnership program around Codex—Accenture, PwC, Infosys, and others are joining forces to deploy AI into the software development lifecycle.</strong> From my perspective: this is an important step towards <strong>mass professionalization of generative AI deployments in enterprise IT</strong>. Global consultants don’t enter these spaces without proven use cases and reference clients.</p>

<h2>Codex Transformation Partners – What Does It Mean in Practice?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Scaling Codex deployments within large organizations.</strong> Partners are expected to adapt, integrate, and optimize AI across IT ecosystems worldwide, not just in pilot teams.</li>
<li><strong>Comprehensive services:</strong> from coding automation to refactoring, testing, creating documentation, and migration—with support from digital transformation experts who understand enterprise processes, not just AI demos from hackathons.</li>
<li><strong>Security and compliance:</strong> partners are tasked with enforcing standards for security, compliance, and governance. This is essential—as it’s the real blocker for most AI deployments in corporations.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Opportunities and Challenges (Hard Numbers):</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>For business:</strong> Productivity gain in development means a realistic dozens of percent (even 50–70%) acceleration in prototyping, bug catching, generating tests, and documentation. Simple example: a team of 20 developers saves an average of 8 hours per week ("Copilot effect")—that’s about 20 × 8 × 4 weeks × 100 PLN/h = <strong>PLN 64,000 in potential monthly savings</strong>. But…</li>
<li><strong>Challenges:</strong> Input data, legacy code, process automation, team training, and integrations remain the main obstacles. Going back to basics at the start (e.g., cleaning up repositories, defining roles, backups) often takes longer than the AI deployment itself.</li>
<li><strong>Profitability:</strong> ROI appears only after the first few months—the condition is a sensible migration of processes and acceptance from developers. In practice, where there are no change leaders or the team doesn’t see value, AI ends up “in the drawer.”</li>
</ul>

<h2>My Commentary</h2>
<p>We are entering a phase where generative AI is seriously impacting enterprise—not only experimentally, but operationally. I’ve seen similar announcements in past years, but only now, with support from major partners, is broad adoption truly possible. <strong>Key:</strong> don't focus solely on POCs, but genuinely rebuild business processes around Codex’s capabilities.</p>
