<p><strong>OpenAI has just released a case study on how finance teams use Codex to automate labor-intensive processes: preparing MBR (Monthly Business Review), report packages, variance analyses, and scenario planning.</strong> From my perspective: a concrete example of AI in action, exactly where it has the chance to bring the most value—eliminating manual copy-pasting of data and repetitive tasks in finance.</p>
<h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Report generation (MBR, variance bridges):</strong> Codex automates dashboard creation, generates spreadsheets and presentations based on real data from ERP or Excel sheets.</li>
  <li><strong>Checks & balance:</strong> Codex-based scripts detect common errors—e.g., numerical inconsistencies, missing source data, unusual variances.</li>
  <li><strong>Quick scenarios and simulations:</strong> Instead of tedious manual recalculations—you can prompt and instantly get 3–4 versions of forecasts.</li>
  <li><strong>All in natural language:</strong> The "can you code or not?" barrier disappears. An analyst or controller writes what they need, and Codex does the tedious work in the background.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Real numbers—what can be gained?</h2>
<p>
Implementations I managed in finance departments show:<br/>
- <strong>MBR preparation time:</strong> On average 15h/month × 4 people × 80 PLN/h = 4,800 PLN savings monthly (and that's just one report!)<br/>
- <strong>Variance analyses, checks:</strong> Typically 10–30% of a finance team’s time goes to data corrections and manual checking. Automating half of these activities can save 2,000–6,000 PLN monthly.<br/>
For a team of 5–6 people, that’s a real 50–70 hours monthly, which can be reallocated to truly analytical tasks.<br/>
From a controlling or CFO perspective—faster ready data, less “human error,” less stress at month’s end.
</p>
<h2>Opportunities and challenges—an objective look</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Potential:</strong> Speed, improved data quality, lower risk of errors, freeing up the team’s time.</li>
  <li><strong>Roadblocks:</strong> Full automation requires: good, consistent data sources, well-designed processes, and user buy-in (without which AI will just become another “overlay” people bypass in practice). Key: test on small process segments, only on good data, strictly enforce compliance with financial policy.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>From my side:</strong> I’ve already seen similar automation promises fail due to data chaos or lack of team engagement. <strong>The key here: real work on specific business cases and tight feedback from users.</strong></p>
