<h2>Anthropic Report: 81,000 People, Real Needs, Sober Perspective</h2>

<p>Today, Anthropic released the results of analyses of over <strong>81,000 conversations with AI users</strong>. This is not just another PR report – the data comes from real people who already interact with language models in their daily work.</p>

<p><strong>From my perspective</strong> – this scale is impressive, because it makes it possible to better define the directions for development. Too often, AI technology evolves disconnected from the actual needs of businesses and end users.</p>

<h2>What really matters to users?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Reliability of information</strong> – number one on the list. Users want AI that does not "hallucinate" (does not invent or distort facts).</li>
  <li><strong>Confidentiality and privacy of data</strong> – AI must be secure and not transmit confidential materials anywhere.</li>
  <li><strong>Ability to ask precise questions and get explanations</strong> – meaning features like "drill down," follow-up questions, and details.</li>
  <li><strong>Localization and language support</strong> – AI should be effective in Polish as well (in practice – most models still perform mediocrely here).</li>
</ul>

<p>Interestingly, the most frequently mentioned expectations do not include "superintelligence" or automating everything. Instead, there is a focus on <strong>practical, everyday usability</strong>.</p>

<h2>Implementations in practice – where do they "fall short"?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Data and processes</strong>: Even the best AI model won't help if a company doesn't have orderly data sources, clear access rules, or a process for change. The issue of "garbage in data" keeps resurfacing, and rapid implementations rarely fix this.</li>
  <li><strong>Experts and end users</strong>: Strong feedback from people who will actually use AI solutions daily is essential. Without this, it’s hard to talk about real value from an implementation.</li>
  <li><strong>Infrastructure and integrations</strong>: AI that's not well integrated with the company stack simply won’t go into production. Quite often, this requires more investment than the model itself.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What does it deliver?</h2>
<p>Real numbers depend on the size of the company. For example: if you save 15 minutes of work per day on document analysis for 40 people, that’s <strong>15 min × 40 × 20 days = 200 hours per month</strong>. At a labor cost of 60 PLN/hour – that's <strong>about PLN 12,000 per month</strong> just from better data handling. The effect scales when AI actually meets real needs and doesn’t just generate generic summaries.</p>

<h2>Summary – Practical Lessons</h2>
<p>AI doesn’t have to be spectacular – it needs to work, help solve specific tasks, and respond to user needs (just like those in Anthropic’s report). <strong>The key to effective implementation</strong>: precisely defining expectations, continuous feedback, and a sensible data architecture.</p>
