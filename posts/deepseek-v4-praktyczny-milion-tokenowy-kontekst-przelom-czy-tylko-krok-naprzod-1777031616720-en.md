<h2>DeepSeek-V4: Million-Token Context—Finally Usable in Practice</h2><p>Today, Hugging Face announced DeepSeek-V4—a new open-source model that allows AI agents to operate on <strong>context up to one million tokens</strong>. From my perspective, the key point: this is not just a technical demo but a concrete functionality that can be put to real-world use.</p><p>What does that truly mean in practice?</p><ul><li><strong>The model is able to “read” and process enormous datasets</strong>. For instance, you can upload entire documentation, collections of contracts, email databases, or code repositories—without the need to split them into pieces.</li><li><strong>AI agents</strong> can carry out complex operations: search, summarize, and extract data from complete archives (I’ve seen similar announcements in previous years, but until now, the practical limit was much lower).</li><li><strong>A million tokens is about 750,000 words</strong>—imagine processing 6-7 typical books at once, or months’ worth of project logs.</li></ul><h2>Opportunities and Challenges—Technically and Business-wise</h2><ul><li><strong>Opportunity:</strong> Real automation of lengthy processes—e.g., M&A, due diligence, legal analyses, code review. Getting rid of tedious file splitting.</li><li><strong>Challenge:</strong> Cost—processing the whole context demands a lot of RAM and computational power. The cheapest GPUs “can’t keep up,” and an API charge for such an operation could undermine some of the savings if you do the math.<br><em>Simple calculation:</em> If AI replaces 15 hours of a lawyer’s work × 4 people × PLN 250/h = <strong>approx. PLN 15,000 monthly savings</strong>. However, cloud processing costs increase with the number of tokens—so it pays to avoid uploading “data junk.”</li><li><strong>Key here:</strong> Data must be sensibly structured. In my deployments, the pre-cleaning and tagging phase works best—without this, the model gets “lost” in the details.</li><li><strong>Infrastructure:</strong> If you have your own stack (on-prem GPU), you can test DeepSeek-V4 immediately. If you want to use it in production—you need to calculate cloud costs, backup, and potential failovers.</li></ul><h2>What Does This Offer Business?</h2><ul><li><strong>Quick analysis of large datasets</strong>—employee working time cut by even 20-40%.</li><li><strong>Automation of reports</strong>, summaries, and monitoring—for instance, “what changed in hundreds of contracts this month?”</li><li><strong>Development of your own agents</strong> that genuinely “understand” the entire project context, not just fragments.</li></ul><h2>Summary</h2><p>From my side—DeepSeek-V4 makes a big advance in the availability of models with massive context windows. Preparing the data and realistically calculating costs remain crucial. If you want to talk about deploying such a solution—reach out. Let me know in the comments if you see value in using such models in your company.</p>
