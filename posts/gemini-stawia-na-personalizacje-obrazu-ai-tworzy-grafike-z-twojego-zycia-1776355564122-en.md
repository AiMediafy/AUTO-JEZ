<p><strong>The latest Gemini update allows you to generate images that reflect... your life. Google announced that their Nano Banana 2 model can create personalized graphics using user context—including photos and information from Google Photos.</strong> From my perspective, this is a logical step in the evolution of AI assistants. We are moving rapidly from “universal” to “personal” assistants, where personal data forms the foundation of operation.</p>

<h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Image personalization</strong> – The model takes into account your Google Photos and other data from your Google account. As a result, the generated artwork may be closer to real events, people, and places from your life.</li>
  <li><strong>Faster workflow</strong> – The ability to generate personalized visualizations “on demand,” without tedious manual resource searching or working with a designer.</li>
  <li><strong>User context</strong> – Key: the AI understands more than just your prompt, since it has your photo history, locations, and conversations. The algorithm therefore works from a much richer "inspiration base."</li>
</ul>

<h2>Opportunities and Challenges—As Always: Data, Processes, People</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Reduced time spent on visual materials</strong>: Assuming that a custom graphic for an internal report used to take about 45 minutes (searching for photos, minor retouching, emailing with a designer), and with AI this takes 5 minutes— for a marketing team (4 people × 8 graphics per week × 40 minutes difference) it's a savings of about 21 hours per month. With an hourly wage of 70 PLN—over 1,400 PLN per month. At greater scale, the savings grow exponentially.</li>
  <li><strong>Quality and consistency</strong>: Using your own data allows for much greater authenticity of materials—the client “sees themselves” in the communication. In practice, this significantly increases audience engagement.</li>
  <li><strong>Legal and ethical challenges</strong>: Processing your own photos by AI is a sensitive topic—for businesses, this means a need for clear consent policies and data security. From my implementations, it appears that without strong collaboration with legal departments and ongoing audits, nothing will work sustainably in the long run.</li>
  <li><strong>Context management</strong>: A side effect could be potential “information noise”—the larger the data set, the more important clear criteria for selection and prioritization (AI results in this area are... still variable).</li>
</ul>

<h2>Summary</h2>
<p><strong>In summary:</strong> Integrating AI with personal data provides a powerful boost for personalization on a new level—both for users and companies. However, the key remains mastering aspects of data, permissions, processes, and building user trust.</p>
