<p>Google has announced an expansion of Gemini capabilities in Google Sheets. The beta functionality aims to enable the creation, organization, and editing of spreadsheets through simple text commands – from basic operations to advanced data analysis. This is Google's response to increasing expectations regarding AI in the realm of business process automation.</p>  <h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2>  <p>The development of Gemini in Sheets allows, for example, for:</p>  <ul>    <li>Automating basic tasks, such as creating budgets or filtering data, simply by describing what needs to be done.</li>    <li>Analyzing large data sets using simple queries (“Give me a summary of revenue after Q1 2026”).</li>    <li>Developing increasingly complex processes – such as generating dynamic reports based on variable data sources.</li>  </ul>  <p>The Gemini engine operates under the hood as a combination of natural language processing (NLP) and data analysis techniques. In practice, users can focus on describing their needs, while the technical complexity happens in the background.</p>  <h2>Forbes or savings? The numbers speak for themselves</h2>  <p>A tool of this type could have a real impact on work efficiency. Imagine a company employing 10 analysts, each of whom spends 15 hours per week on manually organizing data in spreadsheets. At a rate of 70 zł/hour, that’s nearly <strong>42,000 zł monthly</strong>. Automating these tasks could reduce this time by up to 30–40%, resulting in significant savings.</p>  <p>From my perspective, the key will be the actual adaptation of these solutions to the unique processes of each company.
