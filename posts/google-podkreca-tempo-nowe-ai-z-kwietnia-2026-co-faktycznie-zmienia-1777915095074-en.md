<p><strong>Google has released another batch of AI updates (April 2026)</strong>. Several important announcements: greater emphasis on multimodal models, better tools for developers (Vertex AI, Gemini API), extensions for Workspace, and further integrations with the Google ecosystem. From my perspective – it’s a big move, but the key is translating these announcements into real-world implementations.</p> <h2>What does this change in practice?</h2> <ul> <li><strong>New Workspace features:</strong> Google is developing AI in Gmail, Docs, and other services – including automated summaries, content generation, and better integration with Gemini. In practice, this means time savings for everyday communication or reporting, for example.</li> <li><strong>Vertex AI:</strong> Expansion of the platform for model training and deployment (support for GPT-4, easier pipeline management). For tech teams – less manual work, faster testing, better handling of large data sets.</li> <li><strong>Gemini API:</strong> Facilitates the integration of advanced AI (multimodality – text+image+speech). From my implementations – multimodality does indeed increase efficiency, but it requires well-prepared data and an efficient team onboarding process.</li> <li><strong>Security and control:</strong> Google highlights new tools for auditing and monitoring. Pay attention to compliance – only large-scale organizational testing will show the real value of these tools.</li> </ul> <h2>Real numbers – is it worth it?</h2> <p>From my perspective, companies using Gemini/Workspace can expect noticeable savings – for example, <strong>automating summaries and email generation</strong> is about 10 h per week × 5 people × 70 PLN/h = approx. 14–15 thousand PLN monthly. For larger teams, savings grow proportionally, but the real value becomes visible only when AI supports the entire process (from prompts to API integrations).</p> <h2>Challenges – what to watch out for?</h2> <ul> <li><strong>Data and processes:</strong> Quality data preparation, process consistency, and team onboarding remain critical. AI without good data = low ROI.</li> <li><strong>Infrastructure:</strong> Google's solutions are scalable, but companies need to ensure integrations (API, security, monitoring).</li> <li><strong>Resources and competencies:</strong> Developers need to know the new APIs, while non-technical teams require well-thought-out training programs.</li> </ul> <h2>Summary</h2> <p>Google is showing that it wants to be the leader in business AI – this time with more specifics than hype.
