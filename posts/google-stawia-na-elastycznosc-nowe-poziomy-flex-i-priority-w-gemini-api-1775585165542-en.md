<p><strong>Google announces two new call tiers in the Gemini API: Flex and Priority.</strong> This move clearly shows: there is no single “best” AI model for everyone. The basic choice — pay less and wait longer, or pay more and get instant results — is now becoming much more user-friendly.</p> <h2>What changes in practice?</h2> <ul><li><strong>Flex Tier</strong>: The cheapest option, but with greater variability in response time (latency). The model can be shifted to less utilized resources — expect delays during periods of high traffic. It works well when response time isn’t crucial (e.g. batch processing, generating large reports, idea testing).</li> <li><strong>Priority Tier</strong>: Guaranteed low latency and operational stability at a higher per-call price. If you have a product where seconds matter (e.g., a customer chatbot, interactive search engine), this solution will help minimize “lag” risk even during peak hours.</li></ul> <p>The documentation also reveals a few technical nuances: in the background, there is automatic management of compute allocation, and the model in Priority is constantly “warmed up,” while Flex may experience so-called cold starts. So it’s clear that it’s about real management of cloud resources.</p> <h2>Will this actually bring savings?</h2> <p>Of course — provided you sensibly assign tasks to the appropriate tiers.<br>An example from deployments: if 60% of tasks in your application can wait a few seconds longer and instead of paying $0.20 per “priority” call, you drop to $0.08 on Flex, then with 100,000 such requests per month, you’re saving <strong>$12,000/month</strong> (simplified estimate based on Google API rates from Q2 2026).</p> <p>From my perspective, the key in this model is: <strong>conscious management of processes and data flow</strong>. In practice, many companies use a single service tier “just in case,” because it’s simpler. Here we get a tool that forces (and enables) the division of tasks into those that truly require priority and those that can “wait in line.”</p> <h2>Conclusions</h2> <ul><li><strong>The Gemini API becomes a tool for cost optimization — not just a source of new features.</strong></li> <li>For companies with different types of queries (differing in priority), there is now a real chance to optimize their AI budget.</li> <li>Condition: a detailed analysis of which processes actually require an instant response and which can safely be assigned to Flex.</li></ul> <p>If you’re considering how to cleverly organize AI processes in your company — <strong>let me know</strong> or leave a comment: happy to advise on what can bring a quick return.</p>
