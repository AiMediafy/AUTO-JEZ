<h2>News: OpenAI and Cloudflare — Agent-Based Workflow Gets Serious</h2><p>Today we have a solid announcement: <strong>Cloudflare teams up with OpenAI</strong> and brings the GPT-5.4 and Codex models into its new <strong>Agent Cloud</strong>. What does this mean? Companies can now build, deploy, and scale AI agents faster—automata that handle complex tasks, not just answer questions or write simple code.</p><h2>What does it mean in practice?</h2><ul><li><strong>Access to GPT-5.4 and Codex directly in Cloudflare's cloud</strong> — less headache with AI integration and management, since everything happens on proven infrastructure.</li><li><strong>The ability to create your own AI workflows</strong> — the GPT model no longer just works as "one prompt = one answer," but solves actual <strong>business processes</strong> by automating multi-step tasks.</li><li><strong>Security and scale</strong> — Cloudflare handles security, availability, and performance. This could be a game-changer for companies that have so far hesitated to deploy production AI directly via APIs.</li></ul><p><strong>From my perspective,</strong> the key is opening the door for companies without large ML teams. Now, integrating AI agents like "build an automation for end-to-end order handling" may be closer to real implementations—not just PoCs.</p><h2>Challenges? Sure, there are some</h2><ul><li><strong>Data and context</strong> — For any AI agent to be valuable, it needs access to up-to-date business data. If your systems are closed or fragmented, GPT-5.4 alone won’t solve anything.</li><li><strong>Processes and constraints</strong> — Automating workflows sounds great, but you <strong>need to map out the process</strong>, know where AI truly adds value, and where it could just cause confusion (think complaint handling—not everything can be tied together by an algorithm!).</li><li><strong>People</strong> — Implementing AI changes how teams work. From my deployments: training not just IT staff but all end users is absolutely critical.</li></ul><h2>How much could this bring?</h2><p>A simple example:<br /><ul><li>An AI agent handles 15 requests daily, averaging 30 minutes each.</li><li>That’s 7.5 hours saved per day.</li><li>For four people across 22 working days = <strong>about 660 hours per month</strong>.</li><li>At 70 PLN/hour—that’s up to <strong>45,000 PLN savings/month</strong>.</li></ul>Of course, this is a rough estimate, not including implementation and maintenance costs, but even half that amount is significant.</p><h2>Summary</h2><p><strong>Integrating GPT-5.4 and Codex into Agent Cloud</strong> is a tangible step towards AI-powered process automation at scale. However, to truly extract value from it, companies must "do their homework": better data, organized processes, and trained people.<br />Do you have an idea for automation in your organization or want to talk about the practicalities of deploying AI agents? <strong>Write to me on LinkedIn</strong> or leave a comment—I’m happy to share my experience with implementing such systems.</p>
