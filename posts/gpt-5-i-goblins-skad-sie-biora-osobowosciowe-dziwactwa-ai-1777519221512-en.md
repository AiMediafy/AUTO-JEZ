<p><strong>OpenAI explains where 'goblins' in GPT-5 come from—that is, those oddly personal, sometimes slightly eccentric responses from the model. Why does AI even start 'being itself'? And what does it mean for you if you want to use GPT-5, for instance, in customer service?</strong></p><h2>In short: what's happening?</h2><ul><li>OpenAI released a timeline and analysis of unusual, less predictable model outputs—humorously dubbed 'goblins.'</li><li>This is the result of combining: massive input datasets, fine-tuning, and systemic attempts to give models a 'personality.'</li><li>The team exposed specific causes (e.g. special behavioral tuning vs. unprepared data vs. excess heuristic constraints).</li></ul><h2>From my deployment perspective:</h2><ul><li><strong>The challenge is no longer the AI's knowledge itself, but its consistency and 'human-ness.'</strong> The fact that AI starts to 'live its own life' flows from the huge number of training dependencies and attempts to add 'flavor' to responses. In practice, this means: sometimes your AI will suddenly become more 'playful,' sometimes quite the opposite.</li><li><strong>Root cause:</strong> Most problems arise at the intersection of adapting models to specific entities/brands and complex prompting in multimodal contexts. If you want to fully eliminate 'goblins,' it's not enough to disable jokes or limit input data. You need to work very precisely at the system instruction level and test the model for stability.</li></ul><h2>What does this mean for your business?</h2><ul><li><strong>Risks in B2C relations:</strong> In practice—even the best models, deployed 'cleanly,' can introduce unexpected elements into customer communication. Solution? Regular monitoring, your own tuning (RLHF and manual tests), clear system rules.</li><li><strong>Estimated costs:</strong> If 1 unexpected situation out of 100 interactions translates to 5 minutes of support and costs 10 PLN (at an hourly labor rate of 120 PLN), a company handling 5,000 interactions per month will spend an extra 2,500 PLN on additional fixes (5k × 1% × 10 PLN). These aren't huge amounts, but they scale up in larger volumes and sensitive industries.</li><li><strong>Data and processes:</strong> It's crucial to test the end-to-end user journey and to collect feedback from customers and consultants. I've seen it in practice—sometimes a small AI personality boosts loyalty, and sometimes... it triggers a system error. The difference comes in the feedback loop and organizational culture.</li></ul><h2>To sum up:</h2><p>OpenAI transparently shows the limitations and potential effects of AI 'personality.' From my side—the technology is moving toward increasingly 'human-like' AI, but if you want to deploy it in mass services, account for stability costs and invest in monitoring.
