<p><strong>OpenAI focuses on protection against prompt injection</strong>, which involves manipulating prompts to undesirably alter the behavior of an AI agent. The new approach primarily aims to avoid risky actions and protect sensitive data within agent workflows.</p>  <h2>In practice – what does this mean?</h2>  <p>From a technical perspective, the protections include: </p>  <ul>  <li><strong>Limiting risky actions:</strong> the AI agent operates within predefined rules, reducing the possibility of performing actions not indicated by the user or system.</li>  <li><strong>Securing data:</strong> implementing mechanisms to prevent leakage of sensitive information or access to such data in the event of a successful attack.</li>  </ul>  <p>From the perspective of business implementations, this means greater safety in the use of ChatGPT in business processes – from automating customer service, through data analysis, to supporting managerial decisions. Less risk, more control.</p>  <h2>Why is this important?</h2>  <p>Prompt injection is a real threat. In short, someone can craft a prompt that misleads the model or persuades it to reveal data that should remain hidden. For instance: if your company uses ChatGPT to handle customer queries, such an attack could, for example, extract customers’ personal data or alter responses to incorrect ones.</p>  <p>From experience, I know that AI tools are only as good as their protections. I have witnessed situations where a lack of basic safeguards resulted in severe issues related to data or company reputation. That's why OpenAI’s approach, which considers threats during the design stage, is clearly a step in the right direction.</p>  <h2>What’s next?</h2>  <p>If you’re considering implementing ChatGPT or similar AI agents in your organization, make sure to address security issues in integration projects. Testing for vulnerabilities to such attacks is now an absolute standard.</p>  <p>Find more details in OpenAI’s full post: <a href="https://openai.com/index/designing-agents-to-resist-prompt-injection">Designing AI agents to resist prompt injection</a>.</p>  <p><strong>Let us know in the comments:</strong> what safeguards do you use in your AI implementations?</p>
