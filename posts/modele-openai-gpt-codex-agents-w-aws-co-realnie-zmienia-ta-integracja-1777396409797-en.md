<h2>New: OpenAI Models Straight from AWS</h2><p>Today officially: <a href="https://openai.com/index/openai-on-aws" target="_blank">GPT models (including the new Codex and Managed Agents) are available in AWS</a>. For many companies, this is a tangible change – and from my perspective, an important step towards broad, secure AI-ification of business processes.</p><h2>What Does This Mean on a Technical Level?</h2><ul><li><strong>Direct access to the OpenAI API</strong> from AWS infrastructure (without extra integration on the side of network or permissions, as required when using OpenAI’s public cloud).</li><li><strong>Codex support</strong> – a model specialized for code generation, automation, and script analysis.</li><li><strong>Managed Agents</strong> – ready-made AI agents you can assemble for specific use cases, without having to build everything from scratch.</li></ul><p>From the perspective of a CTO or Head of AI, it’s a <strong>time-saving deployment and reduced legal risk</strong> – data doesn’t need to leave AWS, it’s easier to meet compliance, audit, and security requirements.</p><h2>Opportunities – and Concrete Benefits for Business</h2><ul><li><strong>Faster POCs</strong> – codebase and data are already in AWS, so the first integrations with GPT or agents are a matter of hours, not weeks.</li><li><strong>Security and Compliance –</strong> critical for finance, healthcare, or the public sector. You still need to maintain your own access policies, but the architecture is already aligned with current corporate standards.</li><li><strong>Scalability and Cost –</strong> less traffic between clouds = fewer complications, often lower transfer costs, and easier billing (all on the AWS invoice).</li></ul><p>Example? <strong>IT support automation</strong> (ticketing, classification, suggestions) using Codex + GPT for 3 teams, each with 5 people: previously, dev and integration costs (security review, inter-cloud connections, etc.) were often <strong>~80–100 person-hours</strong>. After this integration – you can realistically bring it down to <strong>~30–40h</strong>. 60 hours × 5 people × 120 PLN/h = <strong>about 36,000 PLN</strong> saved with just one deployment.</p><h2>As Always – Challenges</h2><ul><li><strong>Input data/context</strong> – security doesn’t free you from working on data quality and selection.</li><li><strong>Cost optimization</strong> – easy to “click through” your budget if you don’t optimize inputs and processes.</li><li><strong>People</strong> – technical onboarding of the team, new skills, security-by-design approach.</li></ul><h2>Summary</h2><p>From my experience: <strong>these kinds of integrations really accelerate AI deployments in companies already on AWS</strong>. “Plug & play” is a big simplification, but the real entry barrier is lower. Now’s the time to check where you can gain the most efficiency – it’s worth following these developments closely in 2026.</p><p>Let me know in the comments if you’ve already tested anything on this integration. If you want to talk about deployments (or you don’t know where to start) – feel free to reach out.</p>
