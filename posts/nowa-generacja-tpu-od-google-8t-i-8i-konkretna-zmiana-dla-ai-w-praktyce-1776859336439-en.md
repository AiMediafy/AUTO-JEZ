<p><strong>News of the day:</strong> Google has announced the eighth generation of its Tensor Processing Units—this time with two specialized chips: TPU 8T and TPU 8I. In practice, this is a response to the rapidly increasing requirements of AI agents and large language models.</p><h2><strong>What exactly is changing?</strong></h2><ul><li><strong>TPU 8T</strong> is optimized for training large models—crucial in developing innovative LLM solutions or agent systems.</li><li><strong>TPU 8I</strong> is a version designed for efficient inference—that is, fast processing of user queries, generating responses, and integration of AI into products.</li></ul><p>Google emphasizes that both chips are expected to deliver a significant increase in energy efficiency (reportedly even 2x over previous generations) and scalability—with the ability to configure large clusters.</p><p><strong>From my perspective:</strong> In AI deployments, especially in large enterprises, both the cost of training and the maintenance of low-latency inference can be bottlenecks. The new TPU 8T/8I architecture presents a real opportunity to speed up and optimize these processes—provided that the whole infrastructure (data, deployment processes, DevOps and AI team) is truly ready to operate in the Google cloud.</p><h2><strong>What are the tangible benefits?</strong></h2><ul><li>Lower operational costs: Google claims that inference costs can drop by several dozen percent. For a company generating 10 million queries per month (e.g., chatbot, search)—with a typical cost of 0.01 PLN per query, the savings could be in the range of PLN 20,000–30,000 per month.</li><li>Faster development: Faster model training = quicker testing, shorter time-to-market. A deployment example: simplified model training (e.g., shortening a project by a week) saves a team of 5 people 40 hours each = 200 h × PLN 70/h = ~PLN 14,000 per iterated model.</li><li>New possibilities: Agent-based AI systems (e.g., complex automations in customer service or RPA) can finally operate without limits on speed and response availability.</li></ul><h2><strong>Challenges and what needs to be done for this to actually work?</strong></h2><ul><li>Migrating processes to Google Cloud—updating workflow, data migration, collaboration with DevOps and Security teams.</li><li>Real model optimization for TPUs—requires technical knowledge, testing, and new tools (Google Cloud SDK, TFRT, JAX).</li><li>Preparing the team—training, standardization, and automating deployment processes.</li></ul><p><strong>Conclusion:</strong> The eighth generation of TPUs from Google is a concrete change—a chance for better results, lower costs, and more AI applications. This is not a magical "AI cheaper and faster" button—but for teams prepared to transition to a cloud architecture, it can have a real, measurable impact.</p><p>If you are building AI solutions and want to discuss implementing new infrastructure, write in the comments or in a private message.
