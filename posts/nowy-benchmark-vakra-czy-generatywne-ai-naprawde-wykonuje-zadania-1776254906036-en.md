<p><strong>VAKRA Benchmark – IBM Research and Hugging Face</strong> have presented a new set of tests for generative AI: <a href="https://huggingface.co/blog/ibm-research/vakra-benchmark-analysis" target="_blank">VAKRA</a>. What’s key? We’re moving from judging "pretty answers" to checking whether models can really <strong>complete complex tasks</strong> – not just write code, but carry out a full workflow. From my perspective, this is already a step towards real business deployments.</p><h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2><ul><li><strong>Tasks are multi-stage</strong>, including automation of processes or generating reports based on several data sources.</li><li><strong>Model evaluation focuses on execution activity</strong> – not just output, but checking if AI understands the sequence of steps and knows how to perform them.</li></ul><p>Technically: the tests are approaching the evaluation of typical business workflows, where the key is <strong>the final result’s compliance with the task specification</strong>. VAKRA also accounts for typical AI pitfalls – like skipping parts of instructions or incorrectly aggregating data.</p><h2>Opportunities and challenges</h2><ul><li><strong>Opportunity – more realistic AI evaluation</strong>: Models that perform well on VAKRA can realistically be considered for automating company processes.</li><li><strong>Challenge – data and processes</strong>: I've already seen similar benchmarks falling into the "pretty code" trap. Here, you have to build <strong>clear workflows at the outset, test with diverse data, unlock access to APIs, and limit hallucinations</strong>.</li></ul><h2>What does it offer business?</h2><p>If a model actually completes tasks like automatic data analysis or document generation, <strong>the savings are very tangible</strong>. For example:</p><p><strong>15 h per week × 3 people × 80 PLN/h = approx. 14,000 PLN monthly savings</strong> – that's the number we saw in projects automating documentation.</p><h2>Conclusion</h2><p>From my perspective, VAKRA is a benchmark that sets a new bar. "Pretty answers" are no longer enough – it matters whether AI really <strong>supports process execution</strong>. If you’re planning to implement generative AI in your company, it’s worth starting by checking how your model performs on VAKRA.</p><p><strong>Let me know in the comments what you think</strong> – or write if you want to talk about implementation in your team.</p>
