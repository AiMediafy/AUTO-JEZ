<p><strong>Meta introduces QIMMA: the first qualitative leaderboard for large language models in Arabic on Hugging Face.</strong> We are finally getting benchmarking that addresses the real needs of business and academia in the MENA region—instead of “artificial” results generated for Western text, now we have something strictly designed for the Arabic context. <span style='color: #666;'>(Personally, I see great potential here for fintech, media, and educational institutions.)</span></p>

<h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2>
<ul>
 <li><strong>QIMMA (pronounced “Kima”)</strong> is an open ranking of LLMs based on quality in the Arabic language. Models are tested on real-world prompts, with quality assessment based partly on native speaker evaluations and various source data.</li>
 <li>Levels the playing field: until now, the majority (over 80%) of benchmarks were for English, marginalizing Arabic-speaking markets.</li>
 <li>The project is backed by Meta, TII, and Hugging Face—meaning serious players and implementation practitioners are involved.</li>
 <li><strong>Open source</strong>: the leaderboard is publicly available, so any company or developer can check which model best fits their needs.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Why is this important?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Costs and time</strong>: Implementing “imported” AI (translating prompts, manual fixes) means up to +30% more work during the implementation and maintenance stages—I’ve seen this firsthand in projects in Dubai and Abu Dhabi.</li>
  <li><strong>Precision</strong>: Models evaluated for Arabic perform better with dialects, specific syntax, and local nuances—which is exactly what delivers a true business advantage. The error reduction rate is typically 2–3 times higher than simply using a “generic GPT” and translating.</li>
  <li><strong>ROI</strong>: For example, automating customer contacts in the banking sector—if a local model reduces escalation rates by 20% per month (handling 50,000 cases × 1 min = 830 hours of human services × PLN 75/hour = around PLN 62,000 monthly savings), the investment in the right model pays off in just a few weeks.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Summary:</strong> QIMMA raises the bar for AI quality in languages other than English. Real value arises when companies use benchmarks tailored to their market—instead of copying solutions that only look good “on paper.” The key is: testing on your own data, integration with local tools, and involving native speakers during implementation.</p>
