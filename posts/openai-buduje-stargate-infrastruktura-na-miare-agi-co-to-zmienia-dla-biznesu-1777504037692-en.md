<p><strong>OpenAI has made its intentions clear: they are building infrastructure on a scale never seen before in the AI industry.</strong> As part of the Stargate project, new data centers with power measured in the hundreds of megawatts are being constructed. Their goal is not only to meet today’s demand for large models but—crucially—to lay the foundations for systems approaching the capabilities of AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).</p>

<h2>What exactly is this about?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Stargate</strong> – the flagship new type of infrastructure project from OpenAI, tailored for training and running ultra-large models.</li>
  <li>The expansion includes <strong>new data centers</strong> in the USA, with a planned capacity of over 1000 MW in a several-year horizon (by comparison: a standard large data center is 10-100 MW).</li>
  <li>Key point: <strong>collaboration with Microsoft</strong> in hardware, energy optimization, and cooling.</li>
</ul>

<p>From my experience—every new generation of LLM <strong>requires logarithmically more computing power</strong>. Now we’re talking about infrastructure that will be used by companies processing not hundreds, but millions of tokens daily. In other words: support for massive scale automation, multilingual assistants, complex analysis, or code generation.</p>

<h2>What does this mean for business?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Greater availability</strong> of immense models—not only for tech giants, but also realistically for medium-sized companies, via API/whitelabel.</li>
  <li><strong>Decrease in unit processing costs</strong>—the larger the scale and more optimized the infrastructure, the cheaper each generation or data set becomes.</li>
  <li><strong>Future-proof solutions:</strong> for companies considering their own dedicated models (training/finetuning on sensitive data), this changes the game—the infrastructure of operators like OpenAI could become unbeatable on cost.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>An example of real savings?</strong><br>
Let’s assume an average company implements automation for inquiry handling (chat + CRM). Savings—with correct implementation and data—even <strong>15 h per week × 4 people × 80 PLN/h = 5,000 PLN weekly, or about 20,000 PLN monthly</strong>. If API costs drop even by 30% thanks to new centers—the ROI accelerates by as much as a quarter.</p>

<h2>What challenges lie ahead?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Data and processes</strong>—simply scaling systems won’t help if a company’s workflows are still “analog.”</li>
  <li><strong>Security</strong>—greater scale means higher risks (compliance, privacy, access control). This is still unresolved in many industries.</li>
  <li><strong>Energy</strong>—data centers on this scale present a major challenge for the energy market, including in the context of ESG.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Summary:</strong> OpenAI strongly signals that the future of large models is not only about algorithms, but above all infrastructure—and the ability to leverage its scale. In practice, this translates to <strong>faster, cheaper, and more broadly accessible AI</strong>, provided… business adapts processes and talent to the new possibilities.</p>
