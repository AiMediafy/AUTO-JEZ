<p><strong>OpenAI officially explains why Codex Security does not generate traditional SAST (Static Application Security Testing) reports.</strong> Instead of relying on the classic approach, the company introduces AI-driven constraint reasoning and validation. From my perspective: this is a direction that can genuinely change the dev-sec workflow in larger organizations.</p> <h2>What does the lack of SAST mean in Codex?</h2> <ul><li><strong>No traditional reports</strong> – Codex Security does not return a list of potential holes like “found X issues.”</li> <li><strong>AI checks the code</strong> – instead of relying on rules, Codex Security analyzes the code and searches for real threats, based on constraint reasoning (logic of constraints) and validation (validation of specific cases).</li> <li><strong>Fewer false alarms</strong> – AI focuses on what can actually be a vulnerability, not what is “potentially” a problem.</li> </ul> <h2>In practice for teams:</h2> <ul><li><strong>Time savings</strong> – typical SAST tools generate hundreds of false positives. Estimating: if 4 people on the team spend 10 hours/month each analyzing irrelevant alerts (4 × 10 h × 60 PLN/h ≈ 2,500 PLN/month), AI-driven Codex can reduce this by even half.</li> <li><strong>Change in process approach</strong> – instead of a checklist to tick off, we have interpretation of code and reporting of real issues. The key here: trust in AI and a good feedback loop.</li> <li><strong>New challenges</strong> – the lack of a classic report complicates compliance audits (e.g., PCI DSS, ISO). From deployments I've participated in, preparing clear “AI in the loop” procedures turned out to be crucial so as not to lose trace of vulnerability detection.</li> </ul> <h2>Technology & challenges</h2> <ul><li>Constraint reasoning – AI understands boundaries and dependencies in the code; it doesn’t look for “everything,” instead it checks logic and integrity.</li> <li>Validation – confirms vulnerability based on real scenarios, not just heuristics.</li> <li>It's worth paying attention to <strong>data</strong>: the effectiveness of this class of solutions depends on high-quality input code and correct description of business rules.</li> </ul> <h2>Conclusion</h2> <p>From the perspective of practical deployments, Codex Security is an interesting alternative for companies tired of “alert spam” in classic SAST. However, remember: it’s not a magical system – good processes, documentation, and training for teams are needed.<br> <strong>Let me know in the comments if you’re using AI-driven security or still relying on SAST?</strong></p>
