<p><strong>OpenAI has announced the implementation of Advanced Account Security — an enterprise-grade security package.</strong> In short: a new phishing-resistant login, stronger account recovery procedures, and broader protection of sensitive user data.</p>

<h2>What’s actually changing?</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Phishing-resistant login</strong> – login immune to major social engineering attacks, meaning support for Passkeys, hardware tokens, or advanced two-factor authentication. These are banking-class technologies (WebAuthn/FIDO2).</li>
  <li><strong>Stronger account recovery</strong> – enhanced account recovery. No more simple questions/emails — to regain access, you’ll actually need to confirm your identity (most likely a dedicated KYC flow or device linking).</li>
  <li><strong>Enhanced protections</strong> – extra mechanisms limiting the possibility of account takeover and access to sensitive settings (e.g. changing email, API keys, payments). In practice: even if someone “steals” a login, they won’t be able to do much with it.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>From my perspective: this marks a shift toward serious B2B/B2G deployments.</strong> For many companies and institutions, legality and security concerns are the number one barrier to using AI as a Service. Until now, some compliance branches (especially in banking, insurance, healthcare, or the public sector) had reasons not to implement OpenAI. Now the arguments grow much stronger.</p>

<h2>Concrete business benefits</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Reducing data breach risk</strong>: According to Verizon 2025 research, over 40% of data breach incidents begin with account takeover via phishing. Raising login and recovery standards brings real reduction of these incidents — in a company with 100+ accounts, savings can reach tens of thousands of PLN a year (lost time, investigation costs, reputational security).</li>
  <li><strong>Greater legal comfort</strong>: Easier to justify AI use in accordance with ISO27001, DORA, NIS2 requirements.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What’s worth noting?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Implementation – you’ll need to synchronize company policies with what OpenAI offers. This isn’t a “turn on and forget” but an upgrade of access and onboarding processes.</li>
  <li>Opportunities – finally OpenAI’s AI opens up for critical businesses.</li>
  <li>Challenges – users must accept less “convenient” logins; the key here is training and onboarding.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Summary:</strong> This is a big step toward mature AI deployments in real businesses, especially in demanding sectors. If security was the obstacle in testing ChatGPT or the API — it’s worth revisiting the topic.</p>
