<p><strong>OpenAI is officially launching Privacy Filter – an open model for detecting and redacting personal data in texts.</strong> From my perspective, this is a truly significant move, especially for companies operating in Europe (GDPR) or the US (CPRA, HIPAA), but also for AI teams processing large volumes of text.</p> <h2>What does it mean in practice?</h2> <ul> <li><strong>Privacy Filter detects PII (Personal Identifiable Information)</strong> – that is, names, surnames, addresses, ID numbers, emails, credit card numbers, tax numbers, and other "sensitive" data.</li> <li><strong>Open-weight model</strong> – you can deploy, audit, and host it locally on your own, without risking the privacy policies of partners or clients.</li> <li><strong>State-of-the-art accuracy</strong> – according to OpenAI, this is currently one of the most precise tools of its kind (there are no tests for all languages, but results for English are very good).</li> </ul> <p>Challenges?</p> <ul> <li><strong>It doesn't solve everything</strong> – Privacy Filter provides a solid detection level, but it needs to be integrated with business processes (e.g., an automatic filter before sending emails, a pipeline for analyzing text data).</li> <li><strong>Scaling and local deployment</strong> – it’s key to be able to monitor the filter’s effectiveness and continually train it on your own examples (since personal data come in various formats and industry specifics).</li> <li><strong>Contextual leaks</strong> – simply redacting PII isn’t always enough – sometimes the text reveals identity through context, nicknames, or associations. Vigilance is still needed here.</li> <li><strong>Lack of support for all languages</strong> – the model is trained mainly on English, Polish and other languages require additional work (realistically, you need to invest in your own fine-tuning).</li> </ul> <h2>Real numbers and savings</h2> <p><strong>Automating PII redaction</strong> can reduce manual document analysis time by as much as 70–80%. A project example: 10 hours per week × 5 people × 80 PLN/hour = approximately 16,000 PLN monthly savings – just for one process, e.g., processing support tickets.</p> <p><strong>Risk of GDPR violation penalties</strong> – several PII leak incidents in Poland have resulted in costs of hundreds of thousands of PLN. Privacy Filter is a real barrier worth putting at the "front door" of your systems.</p> <h2>Summary + my perspective</h2> <p>Privacy Filter from OpenAI is a tool you can implement today at minimal infrastructure costs. <strong>The key is</strong>: integration with processes, quality monitoring, possible training on your own data, and user education (not everything can be automated!). From my experience – such a filter is a major step toward practical data security.</p> <p>Let me know in the comments how PII protection works in your company.
