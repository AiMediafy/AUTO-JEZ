<p><strong>OpenAI today launched the Safety Fellowship – a pilot fellowship program for independent researchers working on AI safety and so-called alignment (adapting AI behavior to human expectations and values). From my perspective: a much-needed move if AI is to be more widely deployed in large companies and the public sector.</strong></p>

<h2>What is the program about?</h2>
<p><ul>
  <li><strong>Safety Fellowship</strong> is designed to empower independent specialists to conduct their own research on AI model safety. Funding and substantive support, but without OpenAI imposing research topics.</li>
  <li>The pilot will begin in 2026, and participants will gain access to OpenAI's infrastructure and resources, which significantly increases the chances of breakthrough discoveries.</li>
  <li>Goal: to develop a <strong>new generation of talent in AI safety</strong>. This answers the shortage of experts capable of reliably assessing and improving the safety of the most advanced models.</li>
</ul></p>

<h2>Why does it matter – from a business and organizational perspective?</h2>
<p><ul>
  <li><strong>AI safety</strong> is not just PR – with the growing scale of deployments, inadequate research into alignment translates into real risks (e.g. sensitive data leaks, undesirable behaviors of text and generative models).</li>
  <li>From the perspective of a company implementing AI: the cost of undetected edge cases can be enormous. For example – incidents in chatbots in e-commerce or HR process automation that lead to reputational/financial losses (to put it simply: a single major public mishap – often over 100,000 PLN in losses).</li>
  <li>The industry has been struggling for years with a lack of independent AI auditors. Programs like Safety Fellowship can meaningfully increase the pool of people that companies can turn to for implementations or audits of their systems.</li>
</ul></p>

<h2>Worth noting…</h2>
<p>The program does not immediately change the rules of the game – within companies, it will remain crucial to focus on:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Data quality and security</li>
  <li>Good audit processes (not just on paper, but regular and based on real scenarios)</li>
  <li>Training your own people – because even the best fellows won't solve organizational problems from the "outside"</li>
</ul>
<p>From practical experience: the sooner Polish businesses incorporate audits and consultations with independent experts (including those from such programs), the lower the risk and the better the chances for smooth AI scaling within the company.</p>

<p><strong>To sum up:</strong> OpenAI is taking an important step forward, but the results will take some time. Processes, data, and caution in implementing AI remain essential.</p>
