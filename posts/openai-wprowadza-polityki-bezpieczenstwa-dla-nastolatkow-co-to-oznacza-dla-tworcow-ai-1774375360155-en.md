<p><strong>OpenAI has just announced prompt-based teen safety policies – a set of guidelines for developers using gpt-oss-safeguard.</strong> The goal is to better moderate risks specific to adolescents in AI systems. From my perspective: this is an important step, but it doesn’t end the discussion – concrete actions are needed to truly improve youth safety.</p><h2>What exactly do the new policies cover?</h2><ul><li><strong>The guidelines focus on prompts</strong> – that is, on how the AI model is instructed to respond in risky situations.</li><li>gpt-oss-safeguard is an open-source layer for content moderation – it not only filters, but also allows for adaptation to specific ages.</li><li><strong>Examples of risks:</strong> grooming, encouragement of self-destructive behaviors, profanity, providing harmful information.</li></ul><h2>How does it work – technically, in practice?</h2><ul><li>The AI model has been “taught” to recognize situations involving teenagers and respond according to the policies.</li><li>Prompt-based means moderation isn’t set in stone – it can be tailored to your own application.</li><li><strong>Implementation:</strong> requires integration of gpt-oss-safeguard, prompt updates, and testing the quality of filters.</li></ul><p><strong>Challenges:</strong> Major differences in the level of “sensitivity” among teenagers, the model’s imprecise context recognition, risk of false negatives/positives. In projects I’ve led for edtech & social platforms, the key turned out to be: continuous monitoring + moderator training (not just automation).</p><h2>What’s in it for business?</h2><ul><li>Fewer “hard” bans – more adaptation to local realities.</li><li>Lower costs of incident handling – simple math: if AI detects ~80% of risks, the moderator only needs to handle 20%. If previously it was 15 hours/week × 4 people × 60 PLN/hour = about 14,000 PLN per month, you could save up to 11,000 PLN.</li><li>A chance to comply with EU, US guidelines – in practice: less stress and fewer fines.</li></ul><p><strong>Does this solve the problem?</strong> Not 100%. The policies are an important starting point, but without well-organized processes and monitoring, the team will still have a lot of work to do. What’s crucial here: regular testing, updating prompts, and feedback from young users.</p><p>In summary: OpenAI gives developers tools and guidelines, but implementation requires engagement.
