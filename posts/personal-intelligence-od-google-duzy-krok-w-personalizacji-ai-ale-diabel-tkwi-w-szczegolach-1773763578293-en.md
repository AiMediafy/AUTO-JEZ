<h2>Google announces: Personal Intelligence on a broad scale</h2><p>Google has announced the expansion of the <strong>Personal Intelligence</strong> feature – now not only in Search, but also in the Gemini app and in Chrome. From my perspective, this is one of the most practical AI implementations in recent months, because personalization is key to making these tools genuinely useful.</p><h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2><ul><li><strong>Personal Intelligence</strong> breaks the typical "rigid" logic of search and AI – the services are set to use a user's data (e.g., emails, calendar, documents) to tailor their responses.</li><li>The expansion applies to the <strong>AI mode in Search</strong>, the <strong>Gemini app</strong>, and <strong>Gemini in Chrome</strong>. This means that whether you’re searching in Google, using Gemini for tasks, or browsing the web – the algorithms will remember your preferences, context, and history.</li></ul><h2>Technical aspects and challenges</h2><ul><li>From an implementation standpoint: the most important things are <strong>managing consent, security, and data transparency</strong>. Google assures that users are in control – but in reality, it’s crucial to closely monitor how access management looks, especially within companies.</li><li>Integration of various data sources – this is where the greatest potential lies, but also the biggest challenge. Not every organization has their data in order, and that’s fundamental for Personal Intelligence to work effectively.</li><li>Based on my experience, <strong>proper team training</strong> will be key: AI that collects and analyzes your data requires informed use. Many process errors stem from a poor understanding of what the AI can do and how to configure it.</li></ul><h2>What’s in it for business?</h2><ul><li>The potential time savings are significant – if the tool automatically finds the right document or reminds you about tasks, it’s estimated at <strong>15 min × 20 people × 22 days = approx. 110 working hours per month</strong> saved for an average company. In terms of cost: <strong>110 hrs × 90 zł/hr = approx. 10,000 zł monthly</strong>.</li><li>Better personalization of offers, customer service, and internal processes – a major boost for sales, HR, and support teams.</li></ul><h2>Conclusion</h2><p>Google is moving toward mass personalization – and that’s the right direction. But to extract value, you need to ensure <strong>data order, proper processes, and team education</strong>.
