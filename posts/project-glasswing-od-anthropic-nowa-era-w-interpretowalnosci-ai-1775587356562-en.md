<h2>Project Glasswing – Anthropic launches the AI interpretability project</h2><p>Today Anthropic announced <a href="https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing" target="_blank">Project Glasswing</a> – a new tool (research framework) designed to finally provide real help in understanding how large language models (LLMs) actually work. From my perspective, this is a concrete move towards <strong>greater AI transparency</strong> – and a response to the challenges the industry must solve if AI is to be used in the most sensitive processes across the economy or public administration.</p><h2>What does this mean in practice?</h2><ul><li><strong>Glasswing enables analysis of model "neurons"</strong> – it shows what is responsible for different model behaviors and how these behaviors are connected in so-called "concepts." In other words: we see not just what the model outputs, but also <em>why</em> it does so.</li><li><strong>Works on models like Claude (but not only)</strong> – the framework is designed to support both Anthropic models and open-source models.</li><li>In practice: a scientist/practitioner can check which parts of the code/model get activated for specific prompts, what drives the model’s predictions, and where potential risks may lie (e.g., unintended "hallucinations" or vulnerability to prompt injection).</li><li><strong>Open-source</strong> – available to the community, which speeds up the development of tools for audits and better oversight of AI models.</li></ul><h2>Opportunities vs. challenges</h2><ul><li><strong>Opportunity:</strong> Real interpretability is a must-have for any company implementing AI in regulated industries: banking, insurance, healthcare. Without understanding the model’s mechanics, issues around responsibility and risk emerge.</li><li><strong>Challenges:</strong> Just showing "what lights up inside" is not enough on its own. In implementations I've been part of, the key was not only the tool, but also the culture around working with AI (audit procedures, meaningful KPIs, team training). Glasswing is a good foundation, but the entire surrounding infrastructure is needed.</li></ul><h2>What does this offer to business?</h2><p>Practically: if your AI automates, for example, moderation or document analysis processes (for example, 15 hours per week × 4 people × 60 PLN/hour = about 14,000 PLN monthly savings), interpretability not only provides legal peace of mind but also the ability to precisely "tune" your model. You spot incorrect predictions faster, respond to edge cases, and minimize risk costs.</p><h2>In my opinion</h2><p>Project Glasswing looks like a significant step by Anthropic towards responsible AI. I've seen similar announcements in past years, but here the tool is already available and open source – you can test it right away. What’s crucial will be how quickly practical deployments emerge outside the testing environment. <strong>Worth watching, especially if you’re considering AI for critical processes.</strong></p><p>Let me know in the comments how you approach model interpretability – is it already a must-have in your organization, or just a "nice to have"?</p>
