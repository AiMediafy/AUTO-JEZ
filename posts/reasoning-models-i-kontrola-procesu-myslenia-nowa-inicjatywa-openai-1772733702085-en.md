<p>OpenAI has unveiled a new methodology – <strong>CoT-Control</strong> – which focuses on monitoring and controlling so-called reasoning models, i.e., models deducing 'chains of thought.' With this initiative, the company is taking a step forward in the area of <strong>AI safety</strong>, which is crucial in the development of artificial intelligence.</p> <h2>What does it mean in practice?</h2> <p>Reasoning models (e.g., those based on Chains of Thought techniques, CoT for short) are gaining popularity in areas such as interpreting large data sets, complex planning, or solving multi-step problems. However, their ability to control and self-assess the generated processes is limited.</p> <p>With CoT-Control, OpenAI is testing mechanisms that allow for monitoring the 'path' of the deductive process. In practice, this entails models that:</p> <ul> <li>Are more predictable in generating subsequent steps of analysis.</li> <li>Include built-in safety mechanisms to detect errors or risky suggestions.</li> <li>Facilitate tracking decision processes and conforming to fair use principles in AI.</li> </ul> <p>If this methodology can be effectively implemented, the benefits could be enormous for both science and business.</p> <h2>Real numbers and business context</h2> <p>CoT-Control has potential applications wherever AI is involved in multi-threaded decisions, such as:</p> <ul> <li><strong>Finance:</strong> optimizing credit decisions by analyzing complex user profiles.</li> <li><strong>Logistics:</strong> complex supply chain planning, with AI overseeing operational efficiency and minimizing costs.</li> <li><strong>Law:</strong> AI examining documentation for regulatory compliance, analyzing multi-step connections.</li> </ul> <p>By improving 'thought monitoring,' these models will be able to generate more reliable solutions. In the context of challenges, e.g., incorrect deductions, better control translates directly to lower business risk.</p> <p>Assuming that AI in such processes could replace 3 full-time employees at analysis costs of PLN 100/hour, we are talking about potential savings of <strong>PLN 100–120,000 per year</strong>. And this is just the beginning!</p> <h2>Conclusions and a question for you</h2> <p>From my perspective, CoT-Control is a step in a really good direction, especially in terms of AI safety. Emphasizing transparency in model operation should be the standard, not the exception.</p> <p>It’s worth watching how the technology will develop in practice – particularly where deductive process errors might cost companies a fortune.</p> <p>Let me know in the comments – what applications of such models do you see for your business? Are you already working with something similar?</p>
