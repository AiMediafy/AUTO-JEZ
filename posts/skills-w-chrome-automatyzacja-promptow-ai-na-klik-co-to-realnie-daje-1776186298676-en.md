<p><strong>New from Google: Skills in Chrome</strong> – a tool that lets you turn your best AI prompts into repeatable, one-click operations. It’s a major step toward practical AI automation in everyday work. <strong>What’s the breakthrough? And does it have a real chance to impact company efficiency?</strong></p><h2>What are Skills in Chrome?</h2><ul><li><strong>Skills are saved AI workflows</strong> – you can create a prompt (e.g., email summary, contract analysis, etc.), save it as a Skill and run it whenever you want, without rewriting the instructions each time.</li><li>Possibility of <strong>remixing</strong> (editing and combining) workflows – users can use ready-made solutions and modify them to their needs.</li><li>This works inside the <strong>Chrome browser</strong> – there’s no need to install external tools or configure anything further.</li></ul><p>From my perspective – this is a step in the right direction, because it truly shortens the path from “prompt idea” to “tool used by the team.”</p><h2>Opportunities and challenges – a practical summary</h2><ul><li><strong>Time savings:</strong> for small and medium businesses who often lack their own programmers, this is a way to quickly implement micro-automation. For instance: if the HR department prepares 20 similar summaries per week, and each one takes 15 minutes, a Skill shortens this to a few seconds. <strong>20×15 min = 5 hours weekly × 4 people × 60 PLN/h = around 4800 PLN monthly</strong> – all from just one functionality.</li><li><strong>Prompt standardization:</strong> easy to avoid mistakes or inconsistencies that crop up when everyone uses their own set of instructions.</li><li><strong>Knowledge management in the company:</strong> ready Skills become part of the company’s “process library.” There’s no risk that know-how is limited to just one person.</li><li><strong>Challenge:</strong> <strong>input data quality</strong> and <strong>thoughtful prompt construction</strong>. Automating a poorly designed process = automating an error. For this to make sense, you need to spend a moment on testing and optimizing prompts, then educate your users.</li></ul><h2>What’s next?</h2><p>Google isn’t first here (similar solutions can already be found in certain ChatGPT plugins or no-code tools), but integrating this into Chrome could work at a larger scale. <strong>The key in all this: accurately identifying processes that are truly worth automating.</strong></p><p><strong>Summary:</strong> Skills in Chrome is a tool with vast potential for those who see AI not just as a “gadget,” but a genuine operational advantage. <br>From implementations I’ve led, even small automations can pay off in 1–2 months, if you address a properly identified business pain point.</p><p>Let me know in the comments if you want to see a practical case study, or write if you’d like to discuss implementing AI for your workflows.
